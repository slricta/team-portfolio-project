Juan Luna y Novicio

Juan Luna de San Pedro y 

Novicio Ancheta was a Filipino painter, 

sculptor and a political activist of the Philippine 

Revolution during the late 19th century. 

He became one of the first recognized 

Philippine artists. His winning the gold 

medal in the 1884 Madrid Exposition of 

Fine Arts , along with the silver win of fellow 

Filipino painter Félix Resurrección Hidalgo, 

prompted a celebration which was a major 

highlight in the memoirs of members of 

the Propaganda Movement. Luna painted literary 

and historical scenes, some with an underscore 

of political commentary. His allegorical 

works were inspired with classical balance, and 

often showed figures in theatrical poses.
